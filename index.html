For a long time I thought my problem was discipline.

I'd get focused, build momentum, and then eventually fall back into my old habits. It wasn't for lack of trying. But I just never decided what I was willing to no longer accept from myself.

I had goals. I had massive ambition. But what I didn't have was a set of standards that I refused to negotiate with.

A goal is something you want. A standard is something you refuse to fall below.

And once I stopped chasing goals and started building standards — what I'd accept from myself in terms of how I went through each day, how I handled the emotions of hard days, and what I'd allow in my life — everything slowly started changing.

That's what The Shift is. The exact process I used to figure out what I was actually willing to accept from myself. I designed the ideal version of the life I wanted to live. And lived like it was already true. Consistently. Even when nothing was changing on the outside.

When you do that long enough, you discover a version of yourself you didn't know could exist.
